1. can i get an earlier appointment?


Yes you can get an earlier appointment depending on vaccine availability.
It is important to respect the criteria listed in Question # 2.



2. What are the crirarea for being able to make change my 2nd dose appointment online?


  • You were present at your appointment for your 1st dose.
  • You provided your health insurance number when you booked your first appointment.
  • You provided your correct date of birth.
  • You provided an email address and / or cell phone number to receive notifications of your appointment.



3. When can i get an earlier appointment ?


I can book my earlier appointment 4 weeks (28 days) or more after my first dose.



4. i get an error message saying there's a problem identifying me. what should i do?


  • Check that you entered the right information in each field.
  • Check that your health insurance number and phone number are the same ones you gave when you made your appointment for your first dose (if you're not sure, check your confirmation email to see).
  • I make an appointment COVID-19 Vaccination - Troubleshooting.



5. i am not receiving my validation code, what should i do ?


  • Check to see if it went into your junk mail.
  • Make sure the email address is the one you entered at the time of your first appointment (for example: your email versus your nephew's, your daughter's, or your spouse's).



6. i can't cancel my appointment, what should i do?


To cancel an appointment, consult your appointment confirmation or reminder email and follow the instructions.



7. can i change the type of vaccine for 2nd dose?


For information on how to change the type of vaccine, go to Québec.ca / COVIDvaccine .



8. Travelling and need a vacccine update ?


People who have had two doses of Covishield, or two doses of AstraZeneca vaccine, or a dose of one and a dose of the other, or a dose of one of these vaccines and then a dose of another RNA vaccine (either Pfizer or Moderna) are well protected against COVID-19. In Québec these individuals are considered to be adequately protected, but this is not the case in all countries. Indeed, there is no international consensus on what is valid proof of “adequate vaccination”.



9. in what cases can i change vaccine locations ?


You can now change your 2nd dose appointment to another site in the same region where you received your 1st dose.
